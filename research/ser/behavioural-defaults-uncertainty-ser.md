## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Core Framework — Kahneman & Tversky
Kahneman & Tversky (1979): Prospect Theory — value function asymmetric around reference point; losses weighted approximately twice as heavily as gains. Three heuristics: Representativeness (probability by resemblance), Availability (probability by ease of recall), Anchoring (adjustment from initial value). Tversky & Kahneman (1974, *Science*): Heuristics and biases — confirmed across hundreds of studies. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 2: Individual Differences in Uncertainty Response
Carver & White (1994): BIS/BAS scales measure individual differences in behavioural inhibition (sensitivity to threat/punishment) and behavioural activation (sensitivity to reward). BIS scores predict cautious approach under uncertainty; BAS scores predict bold approach. Scores correlate with observable risk-taking and avoidance behaviour across contexts. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 3: Documentary Observable Patterns
Decision records under uncertainty — historical, archival, judicial — directly reveal: reference point framing (what is treated as the baseline); loss vs gain framing of equivalent outcomes; evidence of anchoring (first stated number or position maintained despite counter-evidence); availability heuristic (vivid examples used as base-rate substitutes). CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for lab conditions; THEORETICAL for archival documentary inference.

### Finding 4: Ambiguity Tolerance
Frenkel-Brunswik (1949): Intolerance of Ambiguity — individual differences in response to ambiguous stimuli. Observable in documentary evidence: premature closure under ambiguous information; black-and-white framing; discomfort language when certainty unavailable. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED construct; MODERATE for documentary inference.

### Contradictions
| Contradiction | Type | Position A | Position B |
|---|---|---|---|
| Loss aversion universality | Empirical | Kahneman & Tversky: universal 2:1 loss-gain ratio | Gal & Rucker (2018): loss aversion overstated; inertia better explains results |
| BIS/BAS as distinct | Empirical | Gray (1982): two independent systems | Joint activation research: BIS and BAS co-activate in complex situations |

### Evidence Gaps
1. No validated method for measuring Prospect Theory parameters from documentary decision records
2. BIS/BAS scale not validated for indirect inference from documentary language

## Transferability Verdict
**VIABLE WITH HEDGING** — Heuristic patterns (availability, representativeness, anchoring, loss framing) are directly observable in documented decisions and communications. BIS/BAS requires translation through regulatory focus linguistic proxies. Prospect Theory parameters require multiple documented decision observations to estimate reliably.
