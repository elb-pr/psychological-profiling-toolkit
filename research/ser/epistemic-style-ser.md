## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Tetlock — Fox/Hedgehog Epistemic Types
Tetlock (1999, 2005): Expert political judgement study (N=284 experts, 82,361 forecasts over 20 years). Foxes (many ideas, integrated) outperform Hedgehogs (one big idea, applied) in long-range forecasting. Linguistic markers: Foxes use more hedging, qualification, probability language, acknowledgement of counter-evidence; Hedgehogs use confident assertion, single-causal framing, ideological templates. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 2: Stanovich — Dysrationalia
Stanovich (2009): *What Intelligence Tests Miss* — introduces dysrationalia: failure to think rationally despite adequate cognitive capacity. Observable in documentary output: cognitive decoupling failure (inability to consider hypotheticals); myside bias (only searching for confirming evidence); contaminated mindware (faulty folk theories applied to problems). These are directly inferable from argument structure in any extended documentary text. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 3: Regulatory Focus Linguistic Signatures
Semin et al. (2005): Promotion-focused epistemic style — abstract language, gain-framing, idealistic; Prevention-focused — concrete language, loss-framing, realistic/cautious. Language abstraction level (Linguistic Category Model — LCM, Semin & Fiedler 1988) measures level of abstraction across noun-verb-adjective hierarchy. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 4: Argument Structure Analysis
Toulmin (1958) argument model — Claim, Data, Warrant, Backing, Qualifier, Rebuttal — provides a formal structure for analysing epistemic quality of documentary output. High-quality epistemic style: explicit warrants, qualifiers present, rebuttals acknowledged. Low-quality: claim without data, no qualifier, no rebuttal. Applied to any written or spoken documentary record. CLASSIFICATION: THEORETICAL for epistemic style profiling; ESTABLISHED for argument analysis in rhetoric.

### Contradictions
| Contradiction | Type | Position A | Position B |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fox/hedgehog stability | Empirical | Tetlock: stable style across domains | Critics: domain-specific; experts hedge in their domain, not others |
| Linguistic abstraction validity | Empirical | Semin et al.: LCM validated in experiments | Naturalistic text: abstraction level confounded by topic and genre |

### Evidence Gaps
1. No published study applying Tetlock fox/hedgehog classification to non-political documentary sources
2. LCM abstraction level not validated for archival documentary inference specifically
