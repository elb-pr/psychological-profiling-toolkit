## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: DERS — Core Validity
Gratz & Roemer (2004): DERS measures six dimensions of emotion dysregulation —
non-acceptance, goals interference, impulse control difficulties, lack of awareness,
limited strategies, lack of clarity [web:225]. Strong internal consistency (α=.80–.93),
convergent validity with depression, anxiety, BPD measures.
DERS-16 (Bjureberg et al., 2016): brief form maintains psychometric properties [web:156].
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED — as a self-report measure

### Finding 2: CRITICAL TRANSFER GAP
DERS is explicitly designed as a self-report instrument. ALL six subscales require
the subject to report on their own internal experience. The instrument CANNOT be
directly applied to documentary sources without subject self-report.
Transfer gap severity: MAJOR for direct DERS application.
This is a finding, not a limitation to paper over. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED

### Finding 3: Gross Process Model as Documentary Bridge
Gross (1998): five-stage process model of emotion regulation — situation selection,
situation modification, attentional deployment, cognitive change, response modulation [web:244].
Each stage has *behavioural observable correlates*:
- Situation selection: approach/avoidance of emotionally activating contexts (observable in decisions)
- Situation modification: attempts to change circumstances (observable in actions)
- Attentional deployment: what subject attends to (observable in what they discuss, ignore)
- Cognitive change: reappraisal vs suppression (observable in language shifts)
- Response modulation: expressive suppression (observable in incongruence between affect and content)
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED as process model; EMERGING as documentary inference framework

### Finding 4: LIWC Emotion Regulation Proxies
Tausczik & Pennebaker (2010): LIWC categories map onto Gross process model stages.
Expressive suppression vs reappraisal detectable in ratio of negative emotion
acknowledgement vs cognitive processing words [web:250].
High emotion word use + low cognitive processing = low regulation (response modulation failure).
CLASSIFICATION: EMERGING — inferred from LIWC correlates not direct DERS validation

### Finding 5: Contradictions
- DERS six-factor structure vs bifactor structure: ongoing debate about whether six
  dimensions or a general dysregulation factor best represents the construct [web:227].
  Nature: EMPIRICAL.
- Gross process model stages not universally accepted as sequential — can be parallel
  and interactive. Nature: EMPIRICAL.

### Evidence Gaps
1. No study validates Gross process model stage inference from documentary sources directly.
2. Expressive suppression especially difficult to detect from text — requires incongruence
   between expressed and apparent emotional state, requiring independent context knowledge.

## Transferability Verdict
VIABLE WITH SIGNIFICANT HEDGING — DERS itself cannot be applied to documentary
sources. The Gross process model provides the documentary inference framework;
DERS dimensions serve as the conceptual target. Skill file must clearly distinguish
between DERS as the validated clinical instrument and Gross process model as the
documentary proxy framework.
Transfer gap: MAJOR for DERS directly; MODERATE for Gross model inference.

## Failure Mode Register
| Failure Mode | Likelihood |
|---|---|
| Applying DERS subscale language to documentary subjects directly | LIKELY to produce invalid inferences |
| Missing expressive suppression (looks regulated but is not) | LIKELY |
| Situation selection inferred without knowledge of available alternatives | POSSIBLE |
| Emotional flatness in formal documents misread as regulation capacity | LIKELY |
