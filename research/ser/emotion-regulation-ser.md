## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Core Framework — Gross Process Model
Gross (1998, *Psychological Review*): Emotion regulation as five-family model: situation selection, situation modification, attentional deployment, cognitive change (reappraisal), response modulation (suppression). Each family has distinct consequences: reappraisal reduces emotional experience and expression without cognitive cost; suppression reduces expression but not experience, with cognitive cost. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 2: Instrument — DERS (Critical Transfer Gap)
Gratz & Roemer (2004, *Journal of Psychopathology*): Difficulties in Emotion Regulation Scale (DERS) — six dimensions: Non-acceptance, Goals, Impulse, Awareness, Strategies, Clarity. SELF-REPORT instrument. **TRANSFER GAP: MAJOR.** DERS cannot be applied to documentary sources because it measures subjective internal experience (awareness, acceptance of emotions) that has no direct behavioural surface form. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for direct assessment; NOT VIABLE for documentary inference directly.

### Finding 3: Gross Process Model — Documentary Transferability
Situation selection: observable from documented choices (what situations entered/avoided). Situation modification: observable from documented action to alter situation. Attentional deployment: partially inferable from what subjects attend to in documented accounts. Cognitive change (reappraisal): directly inferable from how emotional situations are framed and re-framed in documents. Response modulation (suppression): inferable from disconnect between situational stressor and emotional expression in documents. CLASSIFICATION: EMERGING for documentary inference from Gross model components.

### Finding 4: LIWC Emotional Language Proxies
Tausczik & Pennebaker (2010): LIWC emotional language correlates — positive emotion, negative emotion, anger, anxiety, sadness. These provide the documentary proxy for the Expression dimension of emotion regulation: high negative emotion language = lower suppression; low emotional language in high-stressor context = higher suppression. Pennebaker (1997): emotional disclosure in writing associated with health outcomes — emotional expression patterns in naturalistic text are meaningful signals. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Contradictions
| Contradiction | Type | Position A | Position B |
|---|---|---|---|
| Suppression as harmful | Empirical | Gross: suppression has cognitive cost, worse outcomes | Bonanno: situational suppression (regulatory flexibility) is adaptive |
| DERS dimensions as distinct | Definitional | Gratz & Roemer: six-dimensional structure | Factor analyses: 2-3 factor solutions fit equally well |

### Evidence Gaps
1. No validated framework for inferring all six DERS dimensions from documentary sources — this gap is CRITICAL and must be stated in the skill file
2. No study on accuracy of third-party observer inference of Gross process model family from documentary accounts
3. Regulatory flexibility — the adaptive use of multiple strategies — has no established documentary proxy

## Transferability Verdict
**VIABLE WITH SIGNIFICANT HEDGING** — DERS cannot be directly applied to documentary sources. The Gross process model provides the operative framework for documentary inference. Slot 5 (LIWC emotional language) provides the proxy. The skill file must: (1) explicitly replace DERS with Gross process model for documentary inference; (2) flag that Awareness, Clarity, and Non-acceptance dimensions (internal experience dimensions) cannot be inferred from documents without strong corroborating evidence; (3) restrict confident inference to Strategies and Impulse dimensions, which have observable behavioural correlates.

## Documentary Proxy Map
| Gross Family | Observable Signal | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| Situation selection | Documented avoidance/approach patterns in high-emotion contexts | MODERATE |
| Situation modification | Documented efforts to change circumstances before emotion escalates | MODERATE |
| Attentional deployment | What subjects report attending to vs ignoring in documented accounts | LOW |
| Reappraisal | Re-framing of emotional events in subsequent accounts; positive re-evaluation language | MODERATE |
| Suppression | Low emotional expression in documented high-stressor contexts; disconnect between events and affect in accounts | MODERATE |
