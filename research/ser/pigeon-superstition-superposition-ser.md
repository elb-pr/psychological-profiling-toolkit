## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Skinner (1948) — Foundational Mechanism
Skinner (1948, *Journal of Experimental Psychology*): Non-contingent reinforcement produces stereotyped idiosyncratic behaviour — the organism acts as if its behaviour caused the outcome. Full text available: https://psychclassics.yorku.ca/Skinner/Pigeon/ and https://psych.hanover.edu/classes/learning/papers/Skinner%20Superstion%20(1948%20orig).pdf. Core mechanism: temporal contiguity creates perceived contingency even without actual causal link. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 2: Staddon & Simmelhag (1971) — Mechanistic Reanalysis
Staddon & Simmelhag (1971, *Psychological Review*): Reanalysis of Skinner's paradigm — distinguishes terminal behaviour (actual contingency-shaped) from interim behaviour (superstitious). Important for profiling: not all patterned behaviour under uncertainty is superstitious — the S13 framework must discriminate terminal from interim. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 3: Superstitious Key Pecking (PMC 1970) — Replication
PMC (1970): Superstitious key-pecking after peck-produced reinforcements — superstition arises even when reinforcement IS contingent, if schedule creates ambiguity. Confirms superstition is not anomalous but a default condition under uncertainty. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 4: Bradford Hill (1965) — Causal Inference Scoring (SLOT 2)
Hill (1965): Nine criteria for causal inference — Strength, Consistency, Specificity, Temporality, Biological Gradient, Plausibility, Coherence, Experiment, Analogy. PMC (2009): Bradford Hill criteria in the 21st century — confirms criteria remain the standard framework for causal inference in observational contexts. PMC (2005): Counterfactual perspective on Bradford Hill — maps criteria onto counterfactual causal model. Evidence Methods Reviews (2022): systematic review of causal assessment methods — Bradford Hill among most cited and operationally used. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED — directly applicable as the scoring rubric for the wavefunction dimensions.

### Finding 5: Signal Detection Theory — d′ and β
Green & Swets (1966) Signal Detection Theory: d′ measures discriminability between signal and noise; β (or c) measures response criterion. Application to S13: causal belief strength maps onto β (criterion shift); causal discriminability maps onto d′. SDT provides a quantitative framework for scoring where on the superstition-to-signal-detection continuum a subject's causal attribution sits. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED — well-validated in psychophysics; THEORETICAL for the specific application to causal belief assessment from documentary evidence.

### Contradictions
| Contradiction | Type | Position A | Position B |
|---|---|---|---|
| Superstition mechanism | Empirical | Skinner: non-contingent reinforcement sufficient | Staddon & Simmelhag: schedule-induced behaviour explains results without superstition |
| Bradford Hill universality | Paradigmatic | Epidemiologists: criteria are general causal heuristics | Philosophers: criteria conflate multiple distinct causal concepts |
