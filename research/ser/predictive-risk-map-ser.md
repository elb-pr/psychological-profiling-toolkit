## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: HCR-20 — Primary Instrument (DESIGNED FOR INDIRECT USE)
Webster, Douglas, Eaves & Hart (1997): Historical Clinical Risk Management-20 (HCR-20). Structured professional judgement (SPJ) instrument for violence risk assessment. Historical scale (H-items): coded from *case files, records, institutional reports, criminal history* — **no subject presence required for H-scale**. Clinical scale (C-items): requires clinical interview or observation. Risk Management scale (R-items): future context assessment. H-scale alone provides meaningful risk stratification from documentary sources. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED. Douglas, Hart, Webster & Belfrage (2013): HCR-20V3 — updated version with improved dynamic factors. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 2: Meehl (1954) — Actuarial Superiority
Meehl (1954, *Clinical Versus Statistical Prediction*): Actuarial prediction consistently equals or outperforms clinical judgement across domains. Grove et al. (2000) meta-analysis (N=136 studies): actuarial methods outperform clinical in 47% of comparisons, tie in 6%, clinicians win in 47% — overall statistical equivalence with important sub-group differences. Implication: risk profiling from documentary sources should combine actuarial base-rate anchoring with structured professional judgement, not rely on impressionistic clinical inference. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 3: Structured Analytic Techniques — Intelligence Community
CIA/DIA Analysis of Competing Hypotheses (ACH): structured method for evaluating which of multiple competing hypotheses best explains the available evidence. Applies directly to documentary profiling — each psychological hypothesis about subject is evaluated against the full documentary evidence base. Heuer (1999, *The Psychology of Intelligence Analysis*): cognitive bias management in analytical inference. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED (Tier 5 practitioner methodology with documented application history).

### Finding 4: Base Rate and Reference Class Forecasting
Kahneman & Lovallo (1993): Inside view vs outside view — inside view overweights unique features; outside view anchors on reference class base rates. Sunstein & Thaler (2008): Default and choice architecture. For documentary profiling: all trait and risk inferences must be anchored to base rates of the reference class before updating with specific evidence. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Contradictions
| Contradiction | Type | Position A | Position B |
|---|---|---|---|
| Actuarial vs clinical | Empirical | Meehl/Grove: actuarial equivalent or superior | SPJ proponents: structured clinical judgement captures dynamic factors actuarial misses |
| HCR-20 cross-cultural validity | Empirical | Original: validated in Northern European samples | Cross-cultural studies: variable performance in non-Western contexts |

### Evidence Gaps
1. No validation study of HCR-20 H-scale applied to historical figures or archival-only documentary evidence
2. ACH not formally validated against psychological profiling accuracy — practitioner methodology, not peer-reviewed instrument

## Transferability Verdict
**VIABLE** — HCR-20 H-scale is explicitly designed for documentary/archival inference without subject presence. This is the only instrument in the entire toolkit that was built for indirect assessment from records. It provides the integration scaffold for the predictive layer of the profiling system.
