## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Shortcut Learning Framework
Geirhos et al. (2020): deep learning systems rely on spurious correlations (shortcuts)
in training data rather than robust structural features. Human analogy: epistemic style
characterised by surface-feature reliance vs structural reasoning.
Observable in documentary output: argument from salient example vs systematic evidence,
single case generalisation, authority substituting for evidence.
CLASSIFICATION: THEORETICAL — computational analogy to human epistemic style

### Finding 2: Dysrationalia — High IQ, Poor Calibration
Stanovich (2009): dysrationalia = failure to use cognitive capacity for rational
thinking. High-IQ individuals with poor epistemic calibration detectable from:
reasoning errors that persist despite correction, systematic bias in the direction
of preferred conclusions, myside bias (argument construction favouring prior beliefs).
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for the construct; EMERGING for documentary detection

### Finding 3: Fox vs Hedgehog — Epistemic Style Taxonomy
Tetlock (2005): expert political forecasters divided into foxes (draw from diverse
sources, tolerate uncertainty, update readily) and hedgehogs (one big idea, high
confidence, resistant to updating). Foxes outperform hedgehogs in calibrated forecasting.
Observable in documentary output:
- Hedgehog: single explanatory frame applied repeatedly, low uncertainty acknowledgement,
  confident predictions, resistance to disconfirming evidence
- Fox: multiple frameworks, explicit uncertainty, qualified predictions, position updating
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED — validated against objective forecasting outcomes

### Finding 4: Regulatory Focus Linguistic Signatures
Semin et al. (2005) and CORE paper (2010): promotion vs prevention focus produces
distinct linguistic abstraction patterns [web:355][web:358].
Signal discrimination operationally: subjects with high signal sensitivity use
conditional framing, cite sources, distinguish correlation from causation; subjects
with low sensitivity use categorical assertion, anecdote, authority reference.
CLASSIFICATION: EMERGING for epistemic style; ESTABLISHED for regulatory focus language

### Finding 5: Contradictions
- Intelligence vs rationality debate: Stanovich's dysrationalia construct challenged
  by those who argue motivated reasoning is adaptive. Nature: PARADIGMATIC.
- Hedgehog epistemic style may be domain-specific, not global trait. Nature: EMPIRICAL.

### Evidence Gaps
1. No validated instrument for scoring epistemic style from documentary sources.
2. Tetlock's fox/hedgehog coding not published as reproducible scheme.
3. Cultural variation in epistemic style expression in language — not studied.

## Transferability Verdict
VIABLE — epistemic style has direct linguistic surface forms (hedging, qualification,
alternative hypothesis acknowledgement, source citation vs authority assertion).
Fox/hedgehog distinction is particularly well-suited to documentary inference from
public statements, publications, and decision records.
Transfer gap: MINOR for fox/hedgehog distinction; MODERATE for calibration scoring.

| Failure Mode | Likelihood |
