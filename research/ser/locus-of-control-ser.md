## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Core Framework Validity
Rotter (1966) Internal-External Scale: unidimensional I-E spectrum, thousands of
replications. Levenson (1973) IPC model separates Internal, Powerful Others, Chance
into three independent dimensions — better captures the multidimensional reality.
Frontiers editorial (2021): LOC remains among the most studied psychological constructs
globally, with robust predictive validity for health, academic, and occupational outcomes
[web:314][web:317]. Longitudinal stability confirmed over 6-year period [web:318].
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED

### Finding 2: LOC in Language — Direct Documentary Evidence
ACL workshop paper (2017): "Identifying Locus of Control in Social Media Language" —
trained classifiers on Twitter achieve 77% accuracy distinguishing internal vs external
LOC from syntactic and semantic features alone. Key markers: internal LOC correlates
with first-person agency constructions ("I decided", "I chose"); external LOC correlates
with passive voice, environmental attribution, reference to luck/fate/others [web:321].
CLASSIFICATION: EMERGING (single study, social media context only)
FLAG: Single study — treat as EMERGING until replicated.

### Finding 3: Historical Record Application
Zullow, Oettingen, Peterson & Seligman (1988): pessimistic explanatory style (closely
related to external LOC) measured from presidential campaign speeches and historical
texts. Predicts election outcomes and subsequent leader behaviour [web:308].
Content analysis of attribution language in political texts validated against
independent outcome measures. Inter-rater reliability r=.87 [web:311].
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for political leader inference; EMERGING for general subjects

### Finding 4: Contradictions
- Unidimensional (Rotter) vs multidimensional (Levenson) debate: for documentary
  inference, multidimensional model requires more granular coding scheme.
  Nature: DEFINITIONAL.
- LOC stability vs situational specificity: LOC can shift with life events [web:318].
  Documentary snapshot may capture state not trait. Nature: EMPIRICAL.
- Social desirability contamination: externals may express internal language in
  public-facing documents. Nature: EMPIRICAL.

### Evidence Gaps
1. No published inter-rater reliability study for documentary LOC coding specifically
   using Levenson IPC tripartite model (Internal/Powerful Others/Chance).
2. LOC inference from non-verbal behavioural records (not text) — not studied.

## Transferability Verdict
VIABLE — attribution language in text is a validated proxy for LOC orientation.
Transfer gap: MODERATE — ACL study is single, social media context; Zullow et al.
established for political leaders. General documentary subjects require triangulation.
