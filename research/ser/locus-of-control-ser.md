## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Core Framework
Rotter (1966) I-E Scale: individuals differ in generalised expectancy that outcomes are controlled internally (effort, ability) vs externally (luck, powerful others, fate). Levenson (1973) IPC Scale disaggregates external into Powerful Others and Chance — important for forensic profiling where distinguishing deference from fatalism matters. Frontiers editorial (2021, PMC): LOC remains one of the most replicated constructs in psychology, with thousands of independent studies confirming predictive validity for health, work performance, and mental health outcomes. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 2: Indirect Application — Attribution Language
ACL Anthology (2017): *Identifying Locus of Control in Social Media Language* — NLP study showing LOC is expressed in both syntactic structure and semantic content of natural language. Internal LOC markers: agentive verbs, first-person subject position, causal self-attribution. External LOC markers: passive constructions, impersonal subjects, situational attribution. Validated against Rotter I-E scores (r=0.68). CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for linguistic inference.

### Finding 3: Documentary Proxy — CAVE and Explanatory Style
Zullow, Oettingen, Peterson & Seligman (1988): Pessimistic explanatory style in the historical record — applied CAVE to presidential campaign speeches (1948–1984). LOC-adjacent pessimism scores predicted election outcomes above base-rate. Demonstrates LOC-relevant content is recoverable from political documentary sources decades after production. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 4: Longitudinal Stability
PMC 2018 (N=5,000, 6-year longitudinal): LOC is moderately stable over time (test-retest r=0.50–0.65) but shifts with major life events. Implication for profiling: documentary evidence must be dated and sequenced — LOC inference from older documents may not reflect current orientation. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Contradictions
| Contradiction | Type | Position A | Position B |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stability vs malleability | Empirical | Rotter: LOC is stable trait | PMC 2018: significant shift over 6 years |
| Unidimensional vs multidimensional | Definitional | Rotter I-E: single dimension | Levenson IPC: three independent dimensions |

### Evidence Gaps
1. No study applying Levenson IPC (three-dimensional) coding to documentary text — only unidimensional I-E is validated for linguistic inference
2. No forensic-context validation (legal documents, court records) for LOC documentary inference

## Transferability Verdict
**VIABLE** — attribution language in documentary sources is a well-validated proxy for LOC orientation. Unidimensional (I vs E) inference is strong; three-dimensional (IPC) inference requires additional coding protocol.

## Documentary Proxy Map
| Dimension | Primary Signals | Quality |
|---|---|---|
| Internal LOC | Agentive first-person verbs; causal self-attribution; "I decided/chose/made"; personal responsibility framing | STRONG |
| External-Powerful Others | References to authority, hierarchy, gatekeepers as causal agents; deference language | MODERATE |
| External-Chance | Fate/luck/randomness attribution; impersonal causation; "it happened"; passivity markers | MODERATE |
