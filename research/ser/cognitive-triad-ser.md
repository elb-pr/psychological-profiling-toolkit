## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Core Framework Validity
Beck (1967) proposed that depression is maintained by a negative cognitive triad: negative views of self, world, and future. This has been extensively replicated. Haaga, Dyck & Ernst (1991) reviewed 100+ studies and confirmed empirical support for the triad structure, though they noted self and future poles show stronger evidence than the world pole. PMC 2022 adolescent study (N=1,082) confirms all three dimensions are independently predictive of depressive symptoms and distinct from each other. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 2: Instrument — Cognitive Triad Inventory (CTI)
Beckham et al. (1986) developed the CTI as a 36-item self-report measure of the triad. Hollon & Kendall (1980) Automatic Thoughts Questionnaire (ATQ) operationalises the self-pole specifically. Beck Hopelessness Scale (1974) operationalises the future pole. These are self-report instruments. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for direct assessment.

### Finding 3: Validation — Longitudinal and Mediational
PMC longitudinal study (N=256, 3-wave): Cognitive triad mediates hopelessness model — negative future cognition most predictive of suicidal ideation. Cognitive trio study in Han Chinese women (Cambridge, 2013, N=1,970): All three triad dimensions significantly associated with major depressive disorder across cultures.

### Finding 4: Indirect Application — CAVE Technique (CRITICAL SLOT 4/5 SOURCE)
Peterson, Luborsky & Seligman (1983); Seligman et al. (1988, *Journal of Abnormal Psychology*): Content Analysis of Verbatim Explanations (CAVE) — applies explanatory style coding (internal/stable/global) to **any naturally occurring verbatim material**: speeches, interviews, letters, diaries, published writing. Does NOT require subject participation. Validated against direct ASQ (r=0.71). Inter-rater reliability r=0.85–0.92. Applied to historical figures, political leaders, deceased subjects. This IS the documentary proxy method for both the cognitive triad and locus of control. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for indirect documentary application.

### Finding 5: Documentary Proxy — NLP and Text Mining
PMC semantic distance study (2016, N=60): ME–SADNESS and ME–FUTURE semantic distances in word embeddings distinguish depressed from non-depressed subjects in natural writing. Dataset for modelling Beck's cognitive triad from social media (2021): 3,000 Reddit posts coded for self/world/future negativity — NLP models achieve F1=0.72. European Proceedings (2021): Linguistic analysis of automatic thoughts in naturalistic text — grammatical category markers (first-person singular, categorical thinking markers, should-statements) reliably signal cognitive distortions in text. CLASSIFICATION: EMERGING for automated NLP; ESTABLISHED for trained human CAVE coding.

### Contradictions
| Contradiction | Type | Position A | Position B |
|---|---|---|---|
| World pole distinctiveness | Empirical | Haaga et al: world pole weakest | PMC 2022: all three poles independently predictive |
| Triad vs general negative affect | Definitional | Beck: three distinct poles | Critics: single latent negativity factor underlies all three |

### Evidence Gaps
1. No study applies CAVE specifically to the world-pole dimension (environmental negativity) — only self and future poles routinely coded
2. No inter-rater reliability data for documentary triad inference by untrained analysts

## Transferability Verdict
**VIABLE** — CAVE technique is explicitly designed for documentary inference from naturally occurring verbatim material. Self and future poles well-supported. World pole requires additional triangulation.

## Documentary Proxy Map
| Triad Pole | Primary Signals | Evidence Quality |
|---|---|---|
| Self (negative) | First-person singular + failure/inadequacy lexicon; self-blame attribution; minimal self-efficacy language | STRONG |
| Future (negative) | Hopelessness markers; absence of future planning language; fatalistic framing | STRONG |
| World (negative) | Hostile/threatening environmental framing; victim attribution to external circumstances | MODERATE |
