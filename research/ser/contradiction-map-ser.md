## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Kernberg — Splitting as Observable Pattern
Kernberg (1966, 1975): Splitting — inability to integrate positive and negative aspects of self or other — primary defence of borderline and narcissistic personality organisation. Manifests as alternating idealisation and devaluation of same object. In documentary evidence: same individual described in diametrically opposite terms across documents, or within single extended account with no integrative narrative. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED (clinical); EMERGING for documentary inference.

### Finding 2: Narrative Inconsistency Methods
McAdams narrative identity coding (1993, 2001): cross-narrative inconsistency between stated values and documented behaviour is directly codable from life-history narrative. Contradiction between what is claimed (self-narrative) and what is documented (behavioural record) is the primary signal. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 3: Cognitive Dissonance Observable Markers
Festinger (1957): Cognitive dissonance — tension when holding contradictory beliefs or when behaviour contradicts belief. Observable indicators: rationalisation language; post-hoc justification; belief change following documented behaviour change; excessive justification. In documentary evidence: elaborate post-hoc explanation of behaviour inconsistent with prior stated values. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED (experimental); THEORETICAL for documentary inference.

### Finding 4: Psychobiographical Contradiction Analysis
Elms (1994): Psychobiography uses documented contradictions in a subject's life as primary data. Contradiction between public and private behaviour, between stated motive and documented action, between early and late life patterns. Contradiction itself is the psychological signal, not an artefact to be explained away. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED (psychobiographic method, Tier 6).

### Contradictions
| Contradiction | Type | Position A | Position B |
|---|---|---|---|
| Splitting vs context-appropriate variation | Clinical | Kernberg: splitting is pathological | Normal psychology: valuing same person differently across contexts is healthy |
| Dissonance as motivating | Empirical | Festinger: dissonance motivates reduction | Some research: high dissonance tolerance in complex cognition |

### Evidence Gaps
1. No validated coding scheme for distinguishing pathological splitting from healthy contextual variation in documentary accounts
2. No reliability study for cross-document contradiction mapping by non-clinician analysts

## Transferability Verdict
**VIABLE WITH HEDGING** — Contradictions in documentary evidence are directly observable and are themselves primary data. The hedge: distinguishing pathological splitting from healthy contextual variation requires multiple corroborating instances and explicit counter-hypothesis testing. Single contradictions are insufficient — the skill file must require a pattern of at least three documented instances before inferring splitting-level pathology.
