## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Splitting — Core Validity
Kernberg (1966, 1975): splitting defined as active separation of contradictory
object representations — subject alternates between idealisation and devaluation of
same person/institution. Observed in Borderline Personality Organisation (BPO).
Observable in behavioural record as *alternating contradictory positions toward same target*
without acknowledgement of contradiction [web:372][web:377].
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED as clinical construct; EMERGING for documentary detection

### Finding 2: Computational Splitting Detection
UCL computational study (2019): algorithm detects splitting patterns in online text —
sentiment reversals toward same referent over time. Precision=0.74, recall=0.68 [web:229].
Applied to social media data; uses temporal sequence of statements, not single instance.
CLASSIFICATION: EMERGING — single study, social media context, moderate performance

### Finding 3: Trauma and Observable Contradiction Patterns
Trauma and BPD meta-review [web:374]: splitting manifests as observable symptomatic
pattern of contradictory behaviour — idealisation/devaluation cycles, contradictory
self-statements, denial of previously stated positions.
Observable in documentary record: position reversals, attribution reversals (same
event reframed as own achievement vs victim of circumstances), inconsistent
treatment of same-category referents across documents.
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for clinical population; EMERGING for general subjects

### Finding 4: Fragmentation vs Integration — Narrative Coherence
McAdams (2001): narrative identity integration vs fragmentation. Highly integrated
narratives show coherent causal chains, consistent values, reconciled contradictions.
Fragmented narratives show: unexplained shifts, contradictions without resolution,
inconsistent character of self-descriptions [web:249].
AAI coherence scoring (Main, Goldwyn & Hesse): discourse coherence in attachment
narratives rated on consistent/inconsistent scale [web:341].
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for narrative identity measurement; EMERGING for documentary contradiction mapping

### Finding 5: Contradictions
- Splitting as pathological vs as normal ambivalence: all people hold contradictory views;
  splitting is defined by the active separation without acknowledgement. Nature: DEFINITIONAL.
- Temporal contradiction vs semantic contradiction: behavioural record may show change
  over time (normal updating) vs simultaneous holding of contradictory positions
  (splitting). Distinguishing them requires careful temporal analysis. Nature: METHODOLOGICAL.

### Evidence Gaps
1. No validated protocol for scoring splitting specifically from documentary sources
   that are not social media or therapy transcripts.
2. Temporal resolution required to distinguish position updating from splitting not
   established — how much time between contradictory statements constitutes splitting
   vs legitimate belief change?

## Transferability Verdict
VIABLE WITH HEDGING — splitting is detectable in temporal sequences of documentary
material showing contradictory positions toward same referent. Single-document
analysis insufficient; pattern across documents required.
Transfer gap: MODERATE — temporal resolution criterion not established; pathological
vs normal ambivalence distinction requires careful operationalisation.
