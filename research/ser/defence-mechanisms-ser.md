## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Core Framework — Vaillant Hierarchy
Vaillant (1971, 1977) established an empirically derived hierarchy from longitudinal Grant Study data (N=268 men followed 45+ years). Four levels: Psychotic (projection, denial, distortion), Immature (acting out, passive-aggression, idealisation), Neurotic (repression, reaction formation, displacement, intellectualisation), Mature (sublimation, altruism, humour, anticipation). Vaillant, Bond & Vaillant (1986) validated the hierarchy with blind independent raters coding life-history narratives — inter-rater reliability κ=0.79. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 2: Instrument — DMRS (CRITICAL: designed for indirect use)
Perry (1990) Defense Mechanism Rating Scales: observer-rated scale applied to **verbatim interview transcripts or recorded sessions**. Subject is present for the interview but the coding is done by the analyst post-hoc from transcript — not real-time. This design transfers directly to documentary inference from any verbatim account. DMRS-Q (Olson et al., 2021, Frontiers): Q-sort version maintains observer-rated validity, inter-rater reliability ICC=0.82. DMRS-SR-30 (PMC 2020): self-report adaptation, distinct from observer-rated version. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for observer-rated transcript coding.

### Finding 3: Validation — Longitudinal and Adaptive
Vaillant (2013, PMC): Adaptive midlife defence mechanisms predict late-life health outcomes — prospective validation from longitudinal data over 45 years. Longitudinal design means defences coded from historical documents (letters, medical records, interviews) have predictive validity decades forward. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Finding 4: Emotional Control Theory — Observable Communication Markers
Horowitz (1988, PMC): emotional control theory identifies defence categories through **content and form of communications** — what is avoided, what is transformed, the structure of narrative gaps. Directly applicable to any text or transcript. CLASSIFICATION: THEORETICAL but operationally useful.

### Finding 5: Literary/Documentary Defence Coding
Journal Bahtera (2020): DMRS-analogous coding of defence mechanisms from a novel — demonstrates feasibility of defence identification from fictional/documentary narrative without subject presence. European Proceedings (2021): defence mechanisms identified as strategy in conflict situation narrative. CLASSIFICATION: EMERGING for fully indirect documentary application.

### Contradictions
| Contradiction | Type | Position A | Position B |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hierarchy universality | Empirical | Vaillant: universal developmental hierarchy | Cross-cultural critics: hierarchy reflects Western developmental norms |
| Observer vs self-report validity | Empirical | DMRS observer: κ=0.79 | DMRS-SR-30: moderate self-report validity but different construct |

### Evidence Gaps
1. No formal validation study applying DMRS to fully documentary sources (no subject interview) — all DMRS validation uses interview transcripts where subject was present
2. No inter-rater reliability data for DMRS applied to historical/archival documents specifically

## Transferability Verdict
**VIABLE WITH HEDGING** — DMRS is observer-rated from transcripts, making it the closest existing tool to documentary inference. The gap is that DMRS validation used interview transcripts (subject present), not purely archival sources. The hedge: defence coding from documentary sources must acknowledge lower reliability than from interview transcripts.

## Documentary Proxy Map
| Defence Level | Observable Signals | Quality |
|---|---|---|
| Mature (sublimation, humour, altruism) | Constructive reframing; self-deprecating humour; prosocial attribution | MODERATE |
| Neurotic (intellectualisation, displacement) | Abstract/theoretical framing of personal distress; affect redirected to unrelated targets | MODERATE |
| Immature (idealisation, passive-aggression, acting out) | All-or-nothing characterisation of others; indirect hostility; impulsive behavioural accounts | MODERATE |
