## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Dual Process Framework Validity
Kahneman (2011) synthesises decades of heuristics/biases research into System 1
(fast, automatic, heuristic) and System 2 (slow, deliberate, analytic) architecture.
Stanovich & West (2000): individual differences in System 2 engagement account for
15–25% of variance in reasoning task performance independent of IQ [web:362].
Frederick (2005): Cognitive Reflection Test (CRT) is the most widely used individual
differences measure of System 2 engagement — three-item instrument.
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED (framework); EMERGING (documentary inference)

### Finding 2: Observable System 1 vs System 2 Indicators
System 1 observable indicators in documentary output [web:348][web:354]:
- Heuristic language: stereotypic framing, availability anchoring, representativeness
- Confidence without qualification: claims stated as facts without hedging
- Consistency with first impressions: no self-correction, no position updating
- Emotionally loaded language drives conclusions

System 2 observable indicators:
- Explicit self-correction and qualification
- Enumeration of alternative hypotheses
- Explicit acknowledgement of uncertainty
- Conditional framing ("if X, then Y; however if Z...")
- Separation of data from interpretation in writing structure
CLASSIFICATION: THEORETICAL — not empirically validated from documentary sources specifically

### Finding 3: Need for Cognition as Proxy
Cacioppo & Petty (1982): Need for Cognition (NFC) scale measures individual tendency
to engage in effortful cognitive processing. High NFC individuals use more complex
sentence structures, more qualifiers, more counterargument acknowledgement in their
output. Validated across text and speech samples.
CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for NFC; EMERGING as proxy for System 2 engagement

### Finding 4: Dual Process — Criticisms and Alternatives
Multiple papers challenge the clean System 1/2 binary [web:347][web:354]:
- Spectrum model: processing is a continuum, not two discrete systems
- Conditioning and implicit processes not captured by either system (PMC 2019) [web:354]
- Common Model of Cognition (AAAI 2022): S1/S2 as descriptive shorthand not mechanistic
  description [web:349]. Nature: PARADIGMATIC dispute.
CLASSIFICATION: THEORETICAL — framework is useful heuristic with empirical limitations

### Finding 5: Shortcut Learning as Observable Epistemic Style
Geirhos et al. (2020): shortcut learning = reliance on spurious correlations present
in training data. In human terms: epistemic style observable in whether arguments rest
on surface features or deep structure. High shortcut reliance: argument from authority,
surface analogy, single salient example. CLASSIFICATION: THEORETICAL analogy

### Evidence Gaps
1. No validated instrument for scoring System 1 vs System 2 engagement from naturalistic
   documentary text (not experimental tasks).
2. CRT cannot be administered to documentary subjects — no indirect equivalent validated.
3. NFC linguistic proxies not systematically validated in forensic documentary context.

## Transferability Verdict
VIABLE WITH HEDGING — System 1/2 distinction provides a productive theoretical frame
for documentary inference, but no validated scoring protocol exists. Observable output
features (hedging, self-correction, alternative hypothesis acknowledgement) are the
operational proxies. NFC linguistic markers are the closest validated instrument.
Transfer gap: MODERATE — framework is inferential, not directly measurable from documents.
