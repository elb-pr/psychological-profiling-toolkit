## Evidence Synthesis

### Finding 1: Core Framework
Kahneman (2011, *Thinking Fast and Slow*): System 1 — fast, automatic, associative, heuristic; System 2 — slow, effortful, deliberate, rule-based. Stanovich & West (2000, *BBS*): individual differences in System 2 engagement predict rational reasoning performance independent of IQ. Epstein (1994) Cognitive-Experiential Self-Theory (CEST): experiential vs rational processing as stable individual difference. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED for the distinction; EMERGING for reliable individual-level measurement.

### Finding 2: Limitations and Critiques
Melnikoff & Bargh (2018, *TICS*): System 1/2 distinction imprecise — no agreed definition, no clear boundary, multiple incompatible versions in literature. Moving Beyond System 1 and System 2 (PMC 2019): conditioning and implicit processes not captured by either system — spectrum model more accurate. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED that the binary is an oversimplification. Nature of dispute: DEFINITIONAL.

### Finding 3: Observable System 1 Indicators
System 1 in output: heuristic use (availability, representativeness, affect), fast confident judgements without elaboration, belief-consistent processing, error-proneness under load, coherence-seeking narratives. These appear in documentary evidence as: confident assertions without supporting evidence; representativeness-based generalisations; availability heuristic (vivid examples standing for categories); affect-laden reasoning. CLASSIFICATION: THEORETICAL for documentary inference; ESTABLISHED for lab conditions.

### Finding 4: Observable System 2 Indicators
System 2 in output: explicit consideration of alternatives; qualification and hedging; self-correction mid-argument; deliberate probability estimation; expressed uncertainty. Linguistic: epistemic hedge markers (possibly, perhaps, it seems); explicit counter-argument acknowledgement; numerical probability language. CLASSIFICATION: THEORETICAL for documentary inference; ESTABLISHED for structured cognition tasks.

### Finding 5: Regulatory Focus — Linguistic Signatures (SLOT 5)
Semin et al. (2005, VU Research Portal): Linguistic signatures of regulatory focus. Promotion orientation (System 2 approach) produces abstract language, gain-framing, advancement language. Prevention orientation produces concrete language, loss-framing, security language. Validated across 3 experiments. This is the strongest empirically validated linguistic proxy for cognitive orientation style. CLASSIFICATION: ESTABLISHED.

### Contradictions
| Contradiction | Type | Position A | Position B |
|---|---|---|---|
| System 1/2 as dichotomy | Definitional | Kahneman: two systems | Melnikoff & Bargh: single continuous process |
| Stability of processing style | Empirical | Stanovich: cognitive style is stable trait | Evidence base: situational variation substantial |

### Evidence Gaps
1. No validated protocol for coding System 1/2 dominance from documentary text — all research uses task performance, not naturalistic output
2. Regulatory focus linguistic markers validated in experimental settings, not archival documentary inference

## Transferability Verdict
**VIABLE WITH HEDGING** — System 1/2 processing style is inferable from documentary output patterns, but the binary taxonomy is imprecise. The regulatory focus linguistic signature model (Semin et al. 2005) provides the most defensible documentary proxy. The skill file should use regulatory focus language as the primary observable, and System 1/2 as a second-order interpretive frame.
